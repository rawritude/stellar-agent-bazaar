# Game Concept

## Working title

The Velvet Ledger Bazaar

## Premise

You are building a market brand inside a bizarre trade city full of factions, fees, incentives, and nonsense.

The player's main power is not direct action.
The player's power is delegation.
They fund agents, set priorities, choose risk tolerance, approve campaigns, and react to outcomes.

## Design goals

- Show agents doing economic work, not only talking.
- Make money movement part of the story.
- Keep the loop readable and funny.
- Support dramatic mission reports.
- Create systems with enough surprise to feel alive.
- Leave room for real Stellar demo hooks.

## Fantasy pillars

- delegation
- commerce
- uncertainty
- reputation
- humor
- receipts
- consequences

## Player fantasy

The player should feel like a slightly overwhelmed merchant mastermind.
Not all-powerful. Not helpless.
Smart decisions matter, but weird agents and market chaos create memorable stories.

## Why agents belong here

Agents are natural in a market game because they can:
- gather fragmented information
- spend small budgets independently
- perform specialized tasks
- return structured reports
- create different outcomes based on personality and role

That makes them legible to players.
An agent is not abstract AI. It is a little commercial goblin with a budget and an assignment.

## Long-term fantasy

Eventually the player runs a recognizable brand with:
- district reputation
- supplier contracts
- premium product lines
- better analytics
- specialized agents
- political complications
- recurring rivals
- chain-backed paid actions for selected tasks
